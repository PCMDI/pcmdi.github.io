Research Interests

Theoretical studies of climate and atmospheric circulation including climate modeling (GCM's, coupled atmosphere/ocean models, and simplified models), climate change (caused, for example, by changes in atmospheric CO2 and anthropogenic and volcanic aerosols), detection of climate change, paleoclimate (and Milankovitch forcing of climate change), climate sensitivity and processes, unintended consequences of geoengineering, numerical methods, and metrics for gauging model performance.

Professional  Leadership & Recognition

Associate Editor, Journal of Geophysical Research (Atmospheres) 1984-1988.

Member, National Research Council Climate Research Committee, 1995-1998, and Panel on Climate Observing Systems' Status, 1998.

1998 winner (shared as a co-author) of the “Norbert Gerbier – MUMM Award”, given annually by the World Meteorological Organization for our paper entitled “Search for human influences on the thermal structure of the atmosphere,” by Santer, Taylor, et al., Nature, 382, 39-46, 1996.

1998 winner (shared as a co-author) of the “ERL Outstanding Scientific Paper Award”, given by NOAA Environmental Research Laboratories.

Member, NOAA CLIVAR/PAGES Working Group, an advisory group to NOAA's Office of Global Programs (OGP), 2000-2003.

Co-Chair, NCAR Community Climate System Model (CCSM) Climate Change and Assessment Working Group, 2000-2003.

Lead Author, IPCC Working Group I Fourth Assessment Report, Chapter 8: “Climate Models and their Evaluation”, and Contributing Author to Chapters 1 and 10, 2004-2006.  For its four assessments reports, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) shared (with Al Gore) the 2007 Nobel Peace Prize.

Co-winner (as part of a team) of an LLNL Science and Technology Award in recognition of “key contributions to the Scientific Assessment Reports of the IPCC,” 2008.

Co-author of two articles cited by a National Science Digital Library Project as among the twenty “classics” (1824-1995) that have contributed in key ways to our understanding of  climate change, 2008.

Co-coordinator, PMIP (Paleoclimate Modeling Intercomparison Project), 1993-2002; Scientific Steering Committee, 2002-present.

Co-author and developer of the netCDF Climate and Forecast (CF) Metadata Convention (2000-2005); chair, CF "Standards Committee" (2006-present); chair, CF Governance Panel (2006-present).

Chair, Metrics Panel, established by the WCRP Working Group on Numerical Experimentation (2005-2009); also panel member, 2009-present.

Member, Coupled Model Intercomparison Project (CMIP) Panel and ex-officio member of WCRP's Working Group on Coupled Modelling (WGCM) to which the panel reports, 2008-present.

Representative from the Working Group on Coupled Modelling (WGCM) to the WCRP Observation and Assimilation Panel (WOAP), 2009-2011.

Co-winner of an American Meteorological Society Special Group Award "For leadership in implementing, maintaining, and facilitating access to the CMIP3 multi-model dataset archive, which led to a new era in climate system analysis and understanding," 2010.

Ex-Officio Member, IPCC Task Group on Data and Scenario Support for Impact and Climate Analysis (TGICA), 2009-present. 

Co-Chair, NCAR Community Earth System Model (CESM) Climate Variability and Change Working Group, 2010-2012.

Review Editor, Working Group 1 Chapter 12 ("Long-term climate change: Projections, Commitments and Irreversibility") of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change Fifth Assessment Report (IPCC AR5), 2010-2013.

Member, Earth System Documentation (ES-DOC) Steering Committee, 2012-present.
